# Customer Journey Map
## Overview
The customer journey map for kin-portal outlines the phases a user goes through from initial awareness to expansion of the self-hosted service platform. This map highlights trigger events, friction points, user emotions, opportunities to delight, and key metrics for each phase.

## Aware
* **Trigger Event**: Family members experience difficulties accessing and managing self-hosted services due to technical barriers.
* **Friction Points**: Lack of technical knowledge, complexity of existing solutions, and fear of data loss or security breaches.
* **User Emotions**: Frustration, confusion, and anxiety about managing self-hosted services.
* **Opportunities to Delight**: Simple, user-friendly interface, clear documentation, and assurance of security and data protection.
* **Metric**: Website traffic, social media engagement, and referrals (Target: 10,000 unique visitors within the first 6 months).

## Consider
* **Trigger Event**: Users discover kin-portal through online search, word of mouth, or social media, and consider it as a potential solution.
* **Friction Points**: Concerns about the learning curve, compatibility with existing services, and cost-effectiveness.
* **User Emotions**: Curiosity, skepticism, and hope for a simpler solution.
* **Opportunities to Delight**: Free trial or demo, customer testimonials, and clear comparison with competitors.
* **Metric**: Number of sign-ups for the free trial, email open rates, and click-through rates (Target: 1,000 sign-ups within the first 3 months).

## Try
* **Trigger Event**: Users decide to try kin-portal, either through a free trial or by purchasing the service.
* **Friction Points**: Technical issues during setup, difficulty in understanding features, and initial disappointment if expectations are not met immediately.
* **User Emotions**: Excitement, anticipation, and slight apprehension about the effectiveness of the solution.
* **Opportunities to Delight**: Prompt and helpful customer support, intuitive onboarding process, and early success stories.
* **Metric**: Trial conversion rates, customer satisfaction surveys, and net promoter score (Target: 25% conversion rate from trial to paid users within the first month).

## Adopt
* **Trigger Event**: Users find kin-portal meets their needs and decide to adopt it as their primary self-hosted service management platform.
* **Friction Points**: Potential for occasional technical glitches, need for ongoing support, and desire for new features.
* **User Emotions**: Relief, satisfaction, and loyalty towards the brand.
* **Opportunities to Delight**: Regular software updates, responsive customer service, and a community forum for feedback and support.
* **Metric**: Customer retention rate, average revenue per user (ARPU), and customer lifetime value (CLV) (Target: 90% retention rate after 6 months, ARPU of $20, and CLV of $100).

## Expand
* **Trigger Event**: Satisfied users explore additional features or services offered by kin-portal, such as premium support or advanced management tools.
* **Friction Points**: Deciding on the value of additional services, potential increase in cost, and ensuring new features align with their needs.
* **User Emotions**: Trust, appreciation for the brand, and eagerness for more solutions.
* **Opportunities to Delight**: Personalized recommendations, special offers for loyal customers, and continuous innovation.
* **Metric**: Upsell and cross-sell rates, net dollar retention, and strategic partnerships (Target: 20% of users adopt at least one additional service within a year, net dollar retention of 120%).

By understanding and addressing the needs, emotions, and pain points at each phase of the customer journey, kin-portal can enhance user experience, increase customer satisfaction, and ultimately drive business growth.
