# Product Requirements Document
## Overview
The kin-portal project aims to create a user-friendly, self-hosted service platform that simplifies access and management for non-technical family members. This document outlines the product requirements, target users, goals, key features, success metrics, and scope for the kin-portal project.

## Problem Statement
Non-technical family members often struggle with managing and accessing various services and devices within their home network. Current solutions are either too complex or require extensive technical knowledge, leading to frustration and decreased adoption.

## Target Users
The primary target users for kin-portal are non-technical family members, including:

* Parents and grandparents who want to easily manage and access services for their family
* Family members with limited technical expertise who need a simple and intuitive interface

## Goals
The kin-portal project has the following goals:

* Provide a user-friendly interface for non-technical family members to access and manage services
* Simplify the process of setting up and configuring services within the home network
* Increase adoption and usage of services among non-technical family members
* Enhance the overall user experience and reduce frustration with current solutions

## Key Features
The following key features are prioritized for the kin-portal project:

1. **Simple and Intuitive Interface**: A user-friendly interface that allows non-technical family members to easily access and manage services
2. **Service Discovery and Configuration**: Automated service discovery and configuration to simplify the setup process
3. **Centralized Dashboard**: A centralized dashboard for managing and monitoring services, including real-time updates and notifications
4. **Access Control and Security**: Robust access control and security features to ensure that only authorized family members can access and manage services
5. **Integration with Popular Services**: Integration with popular services, such as media streaming, file sharing, and smart home devices

## Success Metrics
The success of the kin-portal project will be measured by the following metrics:

* **User Adoption Rate**: The percentage of non-technical family members who adopt and regularly use the kin-portal platform
* **User Satisfaction**: Measured through surveys and feedback, with a target satisfaction rate of 90% or higher
* **Service Uptime and Reliability**: The kin-portal platform will aim for 99.9% uptime and reliability, with minimal downtime and errors

## Scope
The kin-portal project will focus on the following scope:

* **Self-Hosted Platform**: The kin-portal platform will be designed for self-hosting, allowing family members to deploy and manage the platform within their own home network
* **Initial Service Integrations**: The initial release will include integrations with a limited set of popular services, with plans for future expansion and additional integrations
* **User Interface and Experience**: The project will prioritize the development of a simple and intuitive user interface, with a focus on user experience and usability

## Out-of-Scope
The following features and functionalities are out-of-scope for the initial release of the kin-portal project:

* **Cloud-Based Hosting**: The kin-portal platform will not be offered as a cloud-based service, and will instead focus on self-hosting within the home network
* **Advanced Networking Features**: Advanced networking features, such as VPN support and custom network configurations, will not be included in the initial release
* **Support for Legacy Devices**: The kin-portal platform will not support legacy devices or outdated operating systems, and will instead focus on modern devices and platforms.
